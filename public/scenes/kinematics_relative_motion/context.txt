Theory:
Relative motion is the calculation of the motion of an object with regard to some other moving or stationary object. All motion is relative to a chosen frame of reference.

Explanation:
If an object A has velocity v_A and an object B has velocity v_B (both relative to the ground), then the velocity of A relative to B is:
v_AB = v_A - v_B

In this simulation:
- The conveyor belt represents a moving frame of reference.
- The projectile is launched from the belt.
- To an observer on the belt, the projectile's horizontal speed is just its launch speed.
- To an observer on the static ground, the projectile's horizontal speed is (Belt Speed + Launch Speed).

Facts:
- There is no "absolute" rest in the universe; everything is moving relative to something else.
- An inertial frame of reference is one where Newton's First Law holds true (it is not accelerating).
- Light is a unique exception: its speed is constant in all inertial frames of reference (Einstein's Special Relativity).

Key Equations:
- v_relative = v_object - v_observer
- v_ground = v_object_relative + v_frame
- x' = x - vt (Galilean Transformation)
