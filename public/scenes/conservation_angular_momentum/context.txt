# Conservation of Angular Momentum

When no external torque acts on a system, its total angular momentum remains constant. This is a fundamental law of physics.

## The Equation
$$L = I\omega = \text{constant}$$

*   **L**: Angular Momentum
*   **I**: Moment of Inertia (rotational mass, depends on how mass is distributed)
*   **ω (omega)**: Angular Velocity (spin speed)

## The Figure Skater Effect
The most common example is a spinning figure skater.
1.  **Arms Out**: Large radius $\rightarrow$ Large Moment of Inertia ($I$). To keep $L$ constant, spin speed ($\omega$) is slow.
2.  **Arms In**: Radius decreases $\rightarrow$ Moment of Inertia ($I$) decreases dramatically.
3.  **Result**: Spin speed ($\omega$) **must increase** to compensate and keep $L$ the same.
