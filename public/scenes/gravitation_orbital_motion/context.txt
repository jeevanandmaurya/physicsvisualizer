# Orbital Motion & Kepler's Laws

Planetary motion is governed by the gravitational force between the planet and the star. Johannes Kepler described this motion with three fundamental laws:

## Kepler's Laws

1.  **The Law of Ellipses**: All planets move in elliptical orbits, with the sun at one focus. This means the distance between the planet and the sun changes constantly.

2.  **The Law of Equal Areas**: A line that connects a planet to the sun sweeps out equal areas in equal times.
    *   **Consequence**: Planets move faster when they are closer to the sun (perihelion) and slower when they are further away (aphelion).

3.  **The Law of Harmonies**: The square of the orbital period of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis of its orbit ($T^2 \propto a^3$).

## Orbital Dynamics

The centripetal force required to keep a planet in orbit is provided by gravity:
$$\frac{mv^2}{r} = \frac{GmM}{r^2}$$

This gives the orbital velocity for a circular orbit:
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$
